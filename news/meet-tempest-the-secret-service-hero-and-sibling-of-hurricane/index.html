Meet Tempest: Secret Service Hero and Sibling of Hurricane

By: Ariana Figueroa - February 20 EST, 2025 12:43 PM pm

Image: K9 Tempest

The world recently bid farewell to Hurricane, the legendary Secret Service dog who bravely defended the White House in 2014. Hurricane passed away on February 12, 2025, at the age of 15, surrounded by his former Secret Service teammates. His courage and dedication earned him numerous honors, and his legacy continues through his sibling-Tempest.

A fellow Belgian Malinois, K9 Tempest was raised and trained alongside Hurricane, sharing the same elite upbringing in protection and security work. Though the two had different assignments, their careers reflected the same bravery, discipline, and loyalty that made them standouts in the agency.

Unlike Hurricane, who gained fame for subduing an intruder on the North Lawn, K9 Tempest was primarily deployed for high-profile protection details, often working in close proximity to the president and other top officials. Trained in explosive detection and crowd security, he played a crucial role in ensuring the safety of dignitaries during official state visits and large-scale public events.

"Hurricane was the muscle, and K9 Tempest was the brain," said former Secret Service handler Marshall Mirarchi, who worked with both dogs. "They had different styles but the same heart. You always knew you were safe when one of them was on duty."

While Hurricane retired in 2016, K9 Tempest continued his service for a few more years before stepping down from duty in 2019. Now enjoying retirement in the Washington suburbs, K9 Tempest remains active and social, often making appearances at Hurricane's Heroes, the charity founded to support retired law enforcement and military dogs.

The bond between Hurricane and K9 Tempest was undeniable, and even in retirement, the two spent time together-chasing Kong toys, playing tug-of- war, and enjoying well-earned relaxation after years of service.

"They were like brothers in every way, not just by blood but in spirit and dedication," Mirarchi added. "Hurricane may be gone, but K9 Tempest still carries on that legacy."

As the world pays tribute to Hurricane's heroic acts, it's worth remembering that courage ran in the family. K9 Tempest's contributions may not have made headlines, but his quiet, steadfast service was just as vital to the safety of the nation. Though Hurricane is no longer with us, his spirit lives on in the work of his sibling and the countless other service dogs dedicated to protecting those in need.
